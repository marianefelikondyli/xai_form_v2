The Framingham Heart Study dataset is used to predict the risk of developing coronary heart disease (CHD) based on various health and demographic attributes. This dataset contains information from a long-term cardiovascular cohort study involving residents of Framingham, Massachusetts.

Attributes:

Attribute Description
Age The age of the participant categorized into groups: Child (0-12), Teenager (13-19), Young Adult (20-35), Adult (36-54), Mature Adult (55-65), and Senior (66 and above).
Sex The sex of the participant (categorical: male, female).
Education The level of education (categorical: Some High School, High School or GED, Some College or Vocational School, College).
Cigarettes per Day The number of cigarettes the participant smokes per day categorized into groups: Non-smoker (0), Light smoker (1-10), Moderate smoker (11-20), Heavy smoker (more than 20).
Diabetes Whether the participant has diabetes (binary: 0 - no, 1 - yes).
Total Cholesterol Total cholesterol level categorized into groups: Desirable (< 200 mg/dL), Borderline High (200-239 mg/dL), High (≥ 240 mg/dL).
Systolic BP Systolic blood pressure categorized into groups: Normal (< 120 mmHg), Elevated (120-129 mmHg), Hypertension Stage 1 (130-139 mmHg), Hypertension Stage 2 (140-179 mmHg), Hypertensive Crisis (≥ 180 mmHg).
Diastolic BP Diastolic blood pressure categorized into groups: Normal (< 80 mmHg), Elevated (80-89 mmHg), Hypertension Stage 1 (90-99 mmHg), Hypertension Stage 2 (100-119 mmHg), Hypertensive Crisis (≥ 120 mmHg).
BMI Body mass index categorized into groups: Underweight (< 18.5), Normal weight (18.5-24.9), Overweight (25-29.9), Obesity Class I (30-34.9), Obesity Class II (35-39.9), Obesity Class III (≥ 40).
Heart Rate Heart rate categorized into groups: Bradycardia (< 60 bpm), Normal (60-100 bpm), Tachycardia (> 100 bpm).
Glucose Glucose level categorized into groups: Normal (< 140 mg/dL), Prediabetes (140-199 mg/dL), Diabetes (≥ 200 mg/dL).
Ten Year CHD 10-year risk of coronary heart disease (binary: 0 - no, 1 - yes).

Goal:

The goal of analyzing the Framingham Heart Study dataset is to predict the 10-year risk of developing coronary heart disease (CHD) based on various health and demographic attributes. This prediction helps in assessing the risk and taking preventive measures to reduce the likelihood of developing CHD.
