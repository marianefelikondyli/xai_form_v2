The German Credit dataset is used to assess the creditworthiness of individuals applying for credit. This dataset contains various attributes related to the demographic and financial information of applicants. Financial institutions use this information to evaluate the risk associated with lending to these individuals.

Attributes:

Attribute Description
Age The age of the individual in years (numeric).
Sex The sex of the individual (text: male, female).
Job The type of job the individual has (numeric: 0 - unskilled and non-resident, 1 - unskilled and resident, 2 - skilled, 3 - highly skilled).
Housing The type of housing the individual resides in (text: own, rent, or free).
Saving accounts The amount of savings the individual has (text: little, moderate, quite rich, rich).
Checking account The amount in the individual's checking account (numeric, in Deutsch Mark).
Credit amount The amount of credit the individual has applied for (numeric, in Deutsch Mark).
Duration The duration of the credit in months (numeric).
Purpose The purpose for which the credit is requested (text: car, furniture/equipment, radio/TV, domestic appliances, repairs, education, business, vacation/others).

Goal:

The goal of analyzing the German Credit dataset is to predict the creditworthiness of individuals based on their demographic information and financial attributes. This prediction helps in assessing the risk posed by the individual when applying for credit, which can be used in decision-making processes within financial institutions.
