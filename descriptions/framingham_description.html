The Framingham Heart Study dataset is used to predict the risk of developing coronary heart disease (CHD) based on various health and demographic attributes. This dataset contains information from a long-term cardiovascular cohort study involving residents of Framingham, Massachusetts.

Attributes:

Attribute Description
Age The age of the participant in years (numeric).
Sex The sex of the participant (binary: male, female).
Education The level of education (ordinal: 1 - some high school, 2 - high school or GED, 3 - some college or vocational school, 4 - college).
Current Smoker Whether the participant is a current smoker (binary: 0 - no, 1 - yes).
Cigarettes per Day The number of cigarettes the participant smokes per day (numeric).
BP Meds Whether the participant is on blood pressure medication (binary: 0 - no, 1 - yes).
Prevalent Stroke Whether the participant has had a stroke (binary: 0 - no, 1 - yes).
Prevalent Hypertension Whether the participant has hypertension (binary: 0 - no, 1 - yes).
Diabetes Whether the participant has diabetes (binary: 0 - no, 1 - yes).
Total Cholesterol Total cholesterol level in mg/dL (numeric).
Systolic BP Systolic blood pressure in mmHg (numeric).
Diastolic BP Diastolic blood pressure in mmHg (numeric).
BMI Body mass index (numeric).
Heart Rate Heart rate in beats per minute (numeric).
Glucose Glucose level in mg/dL (numeric).
Ten Year CHD 10-year risk of coronary heart disease (binary: 0 - no, 1 - yes).

Goal:

The goal of analyzing the Framingham Heart Study dataset is to predict the 10-year risk of developing coronary heart disease (CHD) based on various health and demographic attributes. This prediction helps in assessing the risk and taking preventive measures to reduce the likelihood of developing CHD.
